Short Bio

I am currently first year master student in Xiamen University. During the first year, I am a visiting student (supervised by Prof. Xin Li) to Louisiana State University (LSU) in which I am engaging 3D reconstruction, image assembly and semantic segmentation researches.
Before this, I received the computer science B.S. degree from Xiamen University in 2017 (Also received economics minor B.S. degree). My current research focus is on reconstruction and deep learning for high-level vision, especially fragments assembly and video semantic segmentation.

Research Activities

  • 3D reconstruction from sparse RGB-D scanning. (Published)
    • We presented a novel pruning optimization to handle the challenging reconstruction of unordered RGB-D datasets in which inter-frame overlap is small.
  • Image assembly. (On going)
    • We restore the original image from sorts of arbitrary shape fragments by taking advantage of deep learning.
  • Video semantic segmentation for flood elevation detection. (On going)
    • In this project, we intend to automatically detect flood/water elevation from general videos or images.
